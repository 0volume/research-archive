Pitch #14: Self-Improving Multi-Agent Systems with Uncertainty-Aware Oversight

pending 2026-02-28

Summary

Combine evolutionary test-time learning with hierarchical uncertainty metrics to create self-improving multi-agent research system with built-in collapse detection and safe autonomy bounds.

Why This Matters

Our research agent system (research-agent, code-agent, fact-check) operates as a multi-agent team. Two critical challenges emerge:

Problem 1: Agents can't improve mid-session. Like "clever but clueless interns," they repeat mistakes.

Problem 2: Multi-agent collaboration can converge on wrong conclusions ("debate collapse") without any agent noticing.

Recent research provides solutions to both: EvoTest (evolutionary test-time learning) and hierarchical uncertainty metrics for debate collapse detection.

Full Pitch

The Problem

Current multi-agent research systems face a fundamental limitation: they arrive at deployment with fixed policies and cannot learn from experience within a session. Worse, when multiple agents collaborate (e.g., research-agent + fact-check), they can reach wrong conclusions without any awareness of uncertainty. The Anthropic study on agent autonomy confirms this—experienced users auto-approve more BUT also interrupt more, indicating that even humans struggle to detect when agents go off-track.

Today's Research

Three papers form the foundation:

  • EvoTest (arXiv:2510.13220): Evolutionary test-time learning where an "Actor Agent" executes tasks and an "Evolver Agent" analyzes transcripts to propose improved configurations (prompts, memory, hyperparameters, tool routines). Achieved 38% improvement over strongest baseline.
  • Debate Collapse Mitigation (arXiv:2602.07186): Hierarchical uncertainty metrics at intra-agent, inter-agent, and system levels to detect when multi-agent debate leads to wrong conclusions. Proposes uncertainty-driven policy optimization to penalize self-contradiction and low-confidence outputs.
  • Measuring Agent Autonomy (Anthropic, 2026-02-28): Empirical study of millions of human-agent interactions—found autonomy increasing but human intervention also increasing. Post-deployment monitoring is essential.

Why It Matters for Our System

We have exactly this architecture: research-agent gathers sources, fact-check validates, code-agent implements. When fact-check misses an error or research-agent selects poor sources, the system has no mechanism to:

  • Detect that it's heading toward incorrect conclusions (uncertainty metrics)
  • Improve its approach mid-session based on feedback (evolutionary learning)
  • Know when to escalate to human oversight (autonomy bounds)

Implementation

Phase 1: Uncertainty-Aware Collaboration

  • Implement hierarchical uncertainty scoring for agent outputs (research-agent confidence, fact-check agreement, system-level reliability)
  • Add "collapse detection" - flag when agents converge without sufficient disagreement
  • Build confidence thresholds that trigger human escalation

Phase 2: Evolutionary Learning

  • Add session-level memory that captures successful/failed research strategies
  • Implement "Evolver Agent" role that reviews session transcripts and proposes prompt/budget adjustments
  • Track research success patterns and apply learnings to future queries

Phase 3: Autonomy Bounds

  • Define autonomy tiers based on task risk and uncertainty levels
  • Implement automatic escalation at autonomy boundaries
  • Add post-session analysis for continuous improvement
